A Day in Indianapolis, Indiana

26 Feb 2026

Indianapolis is one of those cities that quietly impresses you. It’s clean, welcoming, walkable, and full of history, food, and fun experiences. From fascinating museums to canal strolls, pedal pubs, monuments, and legendary steakhouses — Indy delivers a full day of adventure.

Morning at the Indiana State Museum

I started my day at the Indiana State Museum, located in a beautiful and lively section of downtown Indianapolis. The museum sits inside White River State Park, which makes the entire area feel open, scenic, and energetic.

The museum itself is fascinating. It dives deep into Indiana’s natural history, cultural development, science, industry, and more. Every exhibit feels thoughtfully curated and immersive. You can easily spend hours exploring and still feel like there’s more to discover.

One of the best parts? When you walk outside, you’re immediately connected to the scenic Downtown Canal Walk.

Strolling the Downtown Canal Walk

The Canal Walk is one of my favorite parts of Indianapolis. It offers a peaceful, relaxing stroll with beautiful water views — but it’s more than just scenery.

Along the path, you’ll find the powerful USS Indianapolis National Monument. The monument tells the tragic story of the USS Indianapolis, its sinking during World War II, and the harrowing shark attacks that followed. It’s sobering, moving, and important history to reflect on.

The canal also connects to the White River and continues through White River State Park, linking to several other museums and attractions — including the nearby Indianapolis Zoo.

If you’re not in the mood to walk, you can rent a pedal boat or even hop into a gondola for a romantic ride along the water. It’s a unique way to see downtown from a different perspective.

Afternoon Fun with The Hangar

Later in the afternoon, I highly recommend heading to The Hangar — especially for their famous HandleBar Pedal Pub Tour.

Yes, it’s exactly what it sounds like.

You and a group hop onto a giant multi-person bike, pedal together, sip drinks, and get an entertaining tour of downtown Indianapolis. It’s energetic, hilarious, and a fantastic way to experience the city. If you’ve ever wanted to drink beer while powering your own moving bar through the streets of Indy — this is your chance.

Exploring Monument Circle

One thing I truly appreciate about Indianapolis is how pristine and well-managed downtown feels. Many cities struggle to keep their downtown areas clean and vibrant — but Indy does it right.

In the heart of downtown sits the iconic Soldiers and Sailors Monument. This towering monument honors the soldiers and sailors from Indiana who were lost in war. It’s both beautiful and meaningful.

Around Monument Circle, you’ll find a lovely public space where people gather, relax, and play games like bag toss. The surrounding area is filled with shops, restaurants, and local businesses.

If you’re looking for something refreshing, I recommend stopping by KOKO TEA for an amazing boba tea. It’s the perfect sweet treat while exploring downtown.

Dinner at a Legendary Steakhouse

To cap off the day, there is one place you absolutely must visit: St. Elmo Steak House.

This legendary steakhouse is famous nationwide — and for good reason. The steak dinner I had there was quite possibly the best I’ve ever experienced. From the quality of the meat to the impeccable service, everything felt classic and elevated.

The restaurant was even featured on the hit TV show Parks and Recreation, further cementing its place in pop culture.

Why Indianapolis Surprised Me

Indianapolis is beautiful, clean, and full of character. Between its rich history, relaxing canal walks, engaging tours, lively downtown atmosphere, and incredible dining scene, it offers something for everyone.

Whether you’re into museums, outdoor strolls, unique group experiences, or unforgettable meals — Indy delivers.

It’s a city full of things to see, do, eat, and drink — and it absolutely deserves a spot on your travel list.
