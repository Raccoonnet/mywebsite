A Day In Cleveland, Ohio

25 Feb 2026

There’s something special about spending a full day exploring a city from sunrise to sunset — and my day in Cleveland was packed with great beer, great food, football, shopping, live music, and a perfect Lake Erie sunset.

Morning at Great Lakes Brewing Company

I started my day bright and early at the legendary Great Lakes Brewing Company. As one of Cleveland’s most iconic craft breweries, it was the perfect way to kick off the morning.

The tour was both fun and educational. I got to learn about the brewing process — from selecting quality ingredients to fermentation and bottling — and hear the story of how this brewery helped shape Cleveland’s craft beer scene. Of course, the best part was sampling several of their craft beers. Each pour had its own personality, from crisp and refreshing to bold and complex.

Right next door is their brewery-owned restaurant, which serves an incredible breakfast. The food was absolutely delicious, and enjoying fresh draft beer alongside a hearty morning meal made for a uniquely Cleveland experience.

Game Time at Huntington Bank Field

After a relaxing and flavorful morning, I headed downtown to Huntington Bank Field, home of the Cleveland Browns.

The energy around the stadium was electric. Browns fans are passionate, loyal, and loud — and the atmosphere did not disappoint. Watching the Browns take the field with Lake Erie in the background is something every football fan should experience at least once. Even better? The Browns won! There’s nothing like celebrating a home-team victory surrounded by thousands of cheering fans.

Afternoon at Legacy Village

After the game, I took a short drive to Lyndhurst to explore Legacy Village.

Legacy Village has a great mix of shops, from clothing boutiques to unique specialty stores and food spots. The outdoor layout makes it especially enjoyable to walk around, and I was lucky enough to catch some live music while browsing. It’s the kind of place where you can easily spend hours just soaking in the atmosphere.

While there, I stopped at Black Box Fix, a unique Black-owned business specializing in Creole and Cajun-inspired sandwiches. Their menu is bold, flavorful, and unlike anything you’ll find at a typical sandwich shop. I ordered one of their incredible creations — packed with spices, sauces, and serious personality.

Sunset on Lake Erie

To end the day, I took my sandwich to the shores of Lake Erie.

Sitting on the beach, enjoying that Cajun sandwich while watching the sun dip below the horizon, was the perfect close to a full Cleveland day. The sky turned shades of orange, pink, and purple, reflecting off the water in a way that makes you slow down and appreciate the moment.

From craft beer and football to shopping, live music, and a lakeside sunset, Cleveland delivered an unforgettable day. If you’re ever looking for a city that blends food, sports, culture, and waterfront beauty — Cleveland should absolutely be on your list.

Thanks for joining me on my quest to visit all 50 states!
