Tennessee: The Soundtrack of the South

Tennessee is a state where the soul of America resonates through its rolling hills, vibrant cities, and small towns steeped in history. From the electrifying rhythms of Nashville’s country music scene to the bluesy streets of Memphis, Tennessee hums with a rich cultural heritage and heartfelt hospitality.

Beyond music, you’ll find breathtaking landscapes in the Great Smoky Mountains, rich Civil War history, and down-to-earth people who invite you to slow down and savor every moment.

Tennessee at a Glance

  • Population: ~6.9 million (2024 estimate)
  • Capital: Nashville
  • Largest City: Memphis
  • Founded: 1796 (Admitted June 1)
  • State Number: 16th state admitted
  • Nickname(s): The Volunteer State
  • Region: Southeastern United States
  • Time Zone: Central Time (CT) and Eastern Time (ET)
  • Area: ~42,143 sq mi (109,247 km²)
  • Climate: Humid subtropical; hot summers, mild winters, occasional snow
  • Major Industries: Music, manufacturing, agriculture, healthcare, tourism
  • Notable Landmarks: Great Smoky Mountains NP, Graceland, Country Music Hall of Fame
  • Transportation: BNA, MEM airports; I-40, I-65; Amtrak
  • Cultural Highlights: CMA Music Festival, Memphis Blues Festival, Civil War sites

Cuisine: Where Flavor Meets Tradition

Tennessee’s food is soulful and hearty — smoky barbecue from Memphis, hot chicken from Nashville, and southern comfort dishes passed down through generations. The state’s culinary landscape is a blend of rich flavors and comforting classics that tell the story of its people.

Culture: The Beat of a Nation

Tennessee’s cultural life pulses with music, history, and storytelling. Whether you’re tapping your feet in Nashville’s honky-tonks or exploring Memphis’ blues roots, the arts are woven into everyday life. Museums, festivals, and historic sites bring to life the state’s vibrant past and dynamic present.

Coffee: Warm Mornings & Strong Brews

Tennessee’s coffee culture blends tradition and trend, from cozy downtown cafés to bustling roasters. The coffee scene reflects the state’s friendly spirit — a place to connect, recharge, and start the day right.

Tennessee: Music, Mountains, and Moments to Cherish

Whether you’re chasing the sound of a guitar, the scent of barbecue, or the peace of mountain trails, Tennessee invites you to experience its soulful blend of tradition and modern life.
