Cave City, Kentucky: Underground Wonder, Small-Town Welcome

Just minutes from Mammoth Cave, Cave City is more than a gateway — it’s a place shaped by time, rock, and good old-fashioned hospitality.

Cave City by the Numbers

  • Population: ~2,200 (2024 estimate)
  • State: Kentucky
  • Founded: 1853
  • Area: ~4.3 sq mi (11.1 km²)
  • Climate: Humid subtropical — hot summers, mild winters, regular rainfall
  • Key Industries: Tourism, recreation, hospitality, small business
  • Notable Landmarks: Mammoth Cave, Wigwam Village, Onyx Cave, Hidden River Cave
  • Transportation:
    • Highways: I-65, US-31W
    • Nearest Airport: Bowling Green–Warren County Airport (~35 mi)
    • Transit: None — car recommended
  • Cultural Highlights: Antique fairs, summer parades, drive-in movies, cave guide legacy

Cuisine: Country Cooking, Done Right

Expect Southern classics, smoky barbecue, and biscuit breakfasts that stick with you all day. Cave City feeds you like family.

Culture: Underground Legends and Local Pride

From historic cave tours to small-town festivals, Cave City’s culture is both natural and neighborly — built on storytelling and shared memory.

Coffee: Cozy Nooks and Small-Town Sips

Simple menus, warm cups, and local connection. Cave City’s coffee scene is small — but heartfelt.

More Than a Gateway

Sure, the caves are legendary — but so is the welcome. Stick around. There’s more to Cave City than you think.
