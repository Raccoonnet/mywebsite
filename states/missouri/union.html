Union, Missouri: Historic Charm with a Warm, Small-Town Spirit

Union offers a wonderful mix of historic charm and community warmth. With roots dating back to the 1800s, this city embraces its heritage while providing a welcoming environment for families, artists, and visitors seeking a slower pace.

From its preserved downtown streets to the nearby scenic trails and vineyards, Union is a place where history and nature meet to create an inviting experience.

City Details at a Glance

  • Population: ~12,000 (2024 estimate)
  • State: Missouri
  • Founded: 1826
  • Area: 10.6 square miles (27.5 km²)
  • Climate: Humid continental — warm summers, chilly winters
  • Key Industries: Agriculture, manufacturing, retail
  • Notable Landmarks:
    • Historic downtown district
    • Bourbeuse River and nearby parks
    • Local vineyards and wineries
  • Transportation:
    • Accessible via Highway 47 and Highway 50
    • Closest major airport: St. Louis Lambert International Airport (STL)
  • Cultural Highlights:
    • Annual festivals celebrating local history and wine
    • Community art events and farmers markets

Cuisine: Comfort Food with Local Flavor

Union’s food scene leans into hearty Midwestern classics and farm-fresh ingredients. Small diners and family restaurants serve dishes that feel like a warm hug, while local wineries add an elegant touch to the culinary experience.

Culture: History and Art in Harmony

The city’s cultural life thrives in its historic sites, art shows, and community events. Local galleries and performances showcase the talents of area artists, while annual events keep the town’s heritage alive.

Coffee: Gathering Spots with Character

Coffee shops in Union invite you to slow down and enjoy. Whether it’s sipping a carefully brewed cup in a cozy café or chatting with locals over pastries, the coffee culture here is warm and genuine.

Experience Union’s Welcoming Spirit

For those seeking a town where history is alive and the community feels like family, Union is a delightful discovery. Take a stroll down historic streets, savor local flavors, and enjoy the peaceful rhythms of small-town life.
