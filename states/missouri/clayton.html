Clayton, Missouri: Sophisticated, Walkable, and Still a Neighborhood

Upscale, urban, but not overwhelming — Clayton finds its balance in quiet confidence and refined charm.

Clayton by the Numbers

  • Population: ~17,400 (2024 estimate)
  • State: Missouri
  • Founded: 1877 (county seat); incorporated 1913
  • Area: ~2.5 sq mi (6.5 km²)
  • Climate: Humid continental — hot summers, cold winters, pleasant springs/falls
  • Key Industries: Legal, finance, corporate HQs, dining, real estate
  • Notable Landmarks: Shaw Park, St. Louis County Courthouse, Clayton Business District
  • Transportation:
    • Major Roads: I-170, Forest Park Pkwy
    • Nearest Airport: St. Louis Lambert (~10 mi)
    • Transit: MetroLink (Clayton Station), Metro Bus, very walkable
  • Cultural Highlights: Saint Louis Art Fair, outdoor concerts, local galleries & events

Cuisine: Global Palates in a 2-Mile Radius

French bistros, ramen joints, and power lunches all share real estate here — and none of it’s phoned in. A small town with serious taste.

Culture: Art Fairs, Park Life, and the Quiet Pulse of the City

Refined, quiet, and deeply rooted — from Shaw Park to gallery rows, Clayton’s cultural life is subtle but satisfying.

Coffee: Work, Relax, Repeat

Where the espresso’s strong, the vibe’s relaxed, and the clientele ranges from entrepreneurs to off-duty teachers. Clayton’s cafés are always dialed in.

Clayton Isn’t Just Polished — It’s Personal

It’s not just the skyline — it’s the sidewalk scenes, the familiar baristas, the kids biking to the park. Clayton gets under your skin — in the best way.
