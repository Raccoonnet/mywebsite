Fenton, Missouri: Where River Views Meet Everyday Living

A down-to-earth town with trails, taverns, and a whole lot of local pride.

Fenton by the Numbers

  • Population: ~4,100 (2024 estimate)
  • State: Missouri
  • Founded: Settled 1818, incorporated 1948
  • Area: ~6.3 sq mi (16.3 km²)
  • Climate: Humid continental — four distinct seasons
  • Key Industries: Manufacturing, retail, logistics
  • Notable Landmarks: Fenton City Park, Meramec Trail, Olde Towne
  • Transportation:
    • Major Roads: I-44, Hwy 141, Gravois Rd
    • Nearest Airport: St. Louis Lambert (~25 mi)
    • Transit: Car-based; walkable in parks/downtown
  • Cultural Highlights: Fenton Days, local concerts, historical exhibits

Cuisine: Comfort Food with River Roots

From family-owned BBQ to old-school diners, Fenton serves up flavors that feel familiar and satisfying.

Culture: Low-Key but Locally Loved

Whether it’s youth sports or fall parades, culture here lives in the everyday — and that’s what makes it matter.
