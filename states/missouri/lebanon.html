Lebanon, Missouri: Route 66 Heartbeat with Ozark Soul

From trout streams to classic diners, Lebanon is a stop worth slowing down for.

Lebanon by the Numbers

  • Population: ~15,000 (2024 estimate)
  • State: Missouri
  • Founded: 1849 (incorporated 1877)
  • Area: ~15.7 sq mi (40.7 km²)
  • Climate: Humid subtropical — four seasons, humid summers
  • Key Industries: Manufacturing, tourism, logistics, education
  • Notable Landmarks: Route 66 Museum, Bennett Spring, Boswell Park
  • Transportation:
    • Major Roads: I-44, Route 66
    • Nearest Airport: Springfield-Branson National (~50 mi)
    • Transit: Car-based with limited local services
  • Cultural Highlights: Route 66 festivals, art guild events, bluegrass nights

Cuisine: Hearty, Local, and Unapologetically Ozark

Whether it’s smoked brisket or scratch pies, Lebanon’s food is built on flavor and familiarity.

Culture: Classic Americana with a Small-Town Spark

Parades, porch music, and the heartbeat of Route 66 keep Lebanon connected to both past and present.
