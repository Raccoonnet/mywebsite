Texas: Vast Lands, Big Stories

Texas is a state where every horizon seems endless, and every town tells a story as big as its skies. From the spirited streets of Austin to the historic Alamo in San Antonio and the sprawling oil fields that powered its rise, Texas is a blend of rugged independence, vibrant culture, and deep-rooted traditions.

It’s a place where barbecue smoke drifts over the hill country, where music festivals bring people together, and where the diversity of landscapes—from deserts to piney woods—invites endless exploration.

Texas at a Glance

  • Population: ~30 million (2024 estimate)
  • Capital: Austin
  • Largest City: Houston
  • Founded: 1845 (Admitted December 29)
  • State Number: 28th state admitted
  • Nickname(s): The Lone Star State
  • Region: South Central United States
  • Time Zone: Central Time (CT) and Mountain Time (MT) in west
  • Area: ~268,596 sq mi (695,662 km²)
  • Climate: Varies widely — arid west, humid east, hot summers, mild winters
  • Major Industries: Energy, tech, agriculture, aerospace, manufacturing, healthcare
  • Notable Landmarks: The Alamo, Big Bend NP, NASA Johnson Space Center, TX State Capitol
  • Transportation: DFW, IAH, AUS airports; I-10, I-35, I-45 highways
  • Cultural Highlights: SXSW, Houston Rodeo, TX State Fair, cowboy heritage

Cuisine: Bold Flavors of the Lone Star

Texas food is bold and diverse — from smoky brisket and spicy Tex-Mex to fresh Gulf Coast seafood. Barbecue pits across the state fill the air with unforgettable aromas, while cities like Austin and Houston offer innovative culinary scenes blending global flavors with local pride.

Culture: Where Heritage Meets Innovation

Texas culture runs deep — cowboy traditions, vibrant music scenes, and arts festivals blend seamlessly with cutting-edge tech hubs and diverse communities. From the rodeos and ranches to the galleries and live music venues, Texas pulses with life and history.

Coffee: Strong Brews in the Lone Star State

Texas’ coffee culture is as varied as its landscapes — from hip Austin cafés brewing locally roasted beans to classic Houston coffee spots where neighbors gather. The community vibe here is warm and welcoming, with plenty of places to savor a strong cup.

Texas: Bigger Than Life, Ready to Welcome You

With its vast landscapes, rich culture, and unmistakable spirit, Texas invites you to explore its stories, tastes, and rhythms — and to make some memories of your own.
