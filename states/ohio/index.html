Ohio: Where Innovation Meets Tradition

Ohio is a place of dynamic contrasts — from bustling cities like Cleveland and Cincinnati to rolling farmland and serene lakeshores along Lake Erie. The Buckeye State balances rich history with a forward-thinking spirit, whether in its museums, music scenes, or thriving industries.

It’s where rock ‘n’ roll legends were born, where hearty Midwestern cuisine fills the table, and where the people are as warm and welcoming as the summer sun.

Ohio at a Glance

  • Population: ~11.7 million (2024 estimate)
  • Capital: Columbus
  • Largest City: Columbus
  • Founded: 1803 (Admitted March 1)
  • State Number: 17th state admitted
  • Nickname(s): The Buckeye State
  • Region: Midwest / Great Lakes
  • Time Zone: Eastern Time (ET)
  • Area: ~44,825 sq mi (116,096 km²)
  • Climate: Humid continental; cold winters, warm summers, lake-effect snow
  • Major Industries: Manufacturing, healthcare, finance, education, tech, agriculture
  • Notable Landmarks: Rock and Roll Hall of Fame, Cuyahoga Valley NP, Pro Football Hall of Fame
  • Transportation: CLE, CMH, CVG airports; Amtrak; I-70, I-71
  • Cultural Highlights: Cincinnati Music Fest, Cleveland Orchestra, Ohio State Fair

Cuisine: A Midwestern Melting Pot

Ohio’s culinary scene is hearty and diverse — from Cincinnati’s famous chili and Polish Boy sandwiches to fresh lake fish and Amish country baking. The state blends Midwestern comfort food with ethnic influences, making every meal a comforting exploration.

Culture: Music, Museums, and More

Ohio’s cultural tapestry weaves rock legends, world-class orchestras, and rich theater traditions. Cities host lively festivals, while small towns preserve folk heritage and crafts.

From the energetic arts scene in Columbus to the historic neighborhoods of Cincinnati and Cleveland, Ohio pulses with creativity.

Coffee: From Cozy Cafés to Trendy Roasters

Ohio’s coffee culture is thriving — independent cafés, local roasters, and welcoming community spots are everywhere. Whether you want a quiet corner to read in Columbus or a bustling café in Cleveland, the coffee scene is rich and inviting.

Ohio: Come for the History, Stay for the Heart

With its blend of tradition and innovation, Ohio welcomes visitors to dive into its stories, tastes, and rhythms. It’s a state where every visit feels like coming home.
